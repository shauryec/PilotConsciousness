01

What I Teach

The focus is the advanced end of the certificate ladder — the training where judgment matters as much as stick-and-rudder, and where good habits either hold or don't.

Instrument
Instrument rating and IPCs. Honest IFR proficiency — flying the system in real conditions, not just to minimums on a good day.
Multi-Engine
AMEL add-on and multi-engine instruction. Engine-out decision-making that stays intact under pressure, not just memorized.
Commercial
The maneuvers to standard. Chandelles, lazy eights, eights-on-pylons, the power-off 180 — precision as a habit, not a stunt.
Endorsements
Complex and high-performance sign-offs, flight reviews, and stage- and checkride prep. Currency turned back into competence.
02

How I Teach

My background is aviation and safety science, and it shapes how I run a lesson. The maneuvers are the vocabulary. The point is the thinking they're built to serve.

›

Scenarios, not just maneuvers

You'll fly situations, not a rote sequence — decisions with real inputs and real consequences, so the reasoning is trained alongside the hands.

›

Read the airplane

The aircraft keeps its own aerodynamic time. I teach you to hear that — to fly by what the machine is telling you and cross-check it against the panel, so instinct and instruments agree.

›

One variable at a time

Energy management as the organizing idea. Reduce a crowded cockpit to the single thing that matters right now — and the workload stops fighting you.

›

Judgment before the moment

The go/no-go, the diversion, the go-around — decided on the ground, loaded before you need them. When the moment comes, you're reading a decision you already made, not making one under pressure.

03

Who It's For

Pilots working toward an instrument, multi-engine, or commercial certificate. Certificated pilots who want real proficiency instead of bare currency. And anyone who'd rather understand the machine than simply operate it — the pilot who wants the record to read competent, not just compliant.
