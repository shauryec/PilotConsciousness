The ecosphere wing

Conscience has to be pre-loaded.

By the time the moment arrives — the airspeed bleeding away, the pressure past where pressure climbs, the conflict already on the scope — there is no time left to build the instrument. It has to already be running. That's the premise of everything taught here.

Who this is for

Anyone with stop-work authority

Flight crews, safety departments, operations leadership, and anyone else whose job includes the authority — formal or not — to say this is wrong and be the reason work stops. The framework was built in a cockpit, but the gap it names is not aviation-specific. It shows up anywhere procedure has quietly replaced judgment.

Formats

TALK

Keynote / conference session

A single-session introduction to the clock framework and the drift — built to reframe how a room thinks about safety culture in under an hour.

WORKSHOP

Half-day or full-day

Working sessions for crews and safety teams: naming the moment conscience gets set down, and rebuilding the habit of reading the record instead of the room.

ADVISORY

Ongoing / embedded

Longer engagements with safety departments and training programs building stop-work culture into procedure, not just policy.

What's taught

Built from the same material as the book

The teaching doesn't simplify the manuscript into slogans. It runs the same instrument the book hands over — the four organs of a regulated system, the difference between the moral filter and real conscience, and the practice of reading a record instead of rehearsing a defense.

Sessions draw directly on Shaurye's instrument time as a Commercial Pilot (ASEL, AMEL, Instrument) and CFI/CFII/MEI, and on his Safety Science graduate work at ERAU Prescott — Safety-II applied to the specific, ordinary moment where a person decides whether to speak.
